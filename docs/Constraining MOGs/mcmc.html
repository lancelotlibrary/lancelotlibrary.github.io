Markov Chain Monte Carlo

In order to perform the N-body simulations for modified cosmologies, we firstly need to constrain every single one of them with the use of various cosmological datasets to determine observables like Ωm0\Omega_{m0}Ωm0​ or H0H_0H0​ and derive the Hubble rate as well as effective gravitational constant GeffG_{\rm eff}Geff​. In our case, we have used Observational Hubble Data (OHD), Supernovae type-Ia distance modulus, Baryon Acoustic Oscillations (BAO) and Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB) datasets to constrain our models. We provide those datasets in a form of a table below.

MCMC methodology usually tries to obtain a best fit for theoretical predictions of distance modulus, H(z)H(z)H(z) by varying the values of input parameters within a given range. As a MCMC sample we have chosen the widely known one, namely emcee. Furtherly, we are going to discuss the results of such MCMC sampling for each modified gravity/neutrino case.

Constraints for regular MOGs#

First kind of modified theory of gravitation that we consider within the current project is modified symmetric teleparallel gravitation (or simply STEGR) with f(Q)=QeλQ0/Qf(Q)=Qe^{\lambda Q_0/Q}f(Q)=QeλQ0​/Q, where λ\lambdaλ is a free model parameter and Q0=6H02Q_0=6H_0^2Q0​=6H02​. Second one is teleparallel gravitation, namely TEGR with f(T)=T+αT(1−epT0/T−T0/pTln⁡(T0/T))f(T)=T+\alpha T (1-e^{pT_0/T}-\sqrt{T_0/pT}\ln\left(T_0/T\right))f(T)=T+αT(1−epT0​/T−T0​/pT​ln(T0​/T)). We also include Non-Minimally Coupled versions of both theories with f1(Q)=Q+αQ2f_1(Q)=Q+\alpha Q^2f1​(Q)=Q+αQ2, f2(Q)=1+βQf_2(Q)=1+\beta Qf2​(Q)=1+βQ and f1(T)=Tf_1(T)=Tf1​(T)=T, f2(T)=1+λ(T/T0)bf_2(T)=1+\lambda(T/T_0)^bf2​(T)=1+λ(T/T0​)b respectively. The last model is Palatini-f(R)f(R)f(R) gravitation, for which we have chosen the following functional form: f(R)=R−2Λ/(μ2/R+1)f(R)=R-2\Lambda/(\mu^2/R+1)f(R)=R−2Λ/(μ2/R+1). Massive neutrino constraints#

As we previously mentioned, apart from the regular case described in the subsection above, we also added massive neutrino species to our MOG models. This can be done by adding non-relativistic neutrino energy density to the total ρm\rho_{\rm m}ρm​:

ρm=ρb+ρc+ρr+ρν\rho_{\mathrm{m}} = \rho_{\mathrm{b}}+\rho_{\mathrm{c}}+\rho_{\mathrm{r}}+\rho_\nuρm​=ρb​+ρc​+ρr​+ρν​

Because of the fact that new species appear, we have also new contribution to the total mass density of the universe:

Ων≈mν93.14h2  eV,mν=∑imνi\Omega_\nu \approx \frac{m_\nu}{93.14 h^2 \mathrm{\;eV}}, \quad m_\nu = \sum_i m_{\nu_i}Ων​≈93.14h2eVmν​​,mν​=i∑​mνi​​
